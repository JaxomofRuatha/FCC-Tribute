Dr. Avram Noam Chomsky

The father of linguistics, and a man of many fields

Dr. Chomsky interviewed in his office at MIT

This is a timeline of Dr. Chomsky's life:

  • 1928 - Born in Philadelphia, PA.
  • 1945 - Begins attending the University of Pennsylvania, studying philosophy, logic, and languages, especially Arabic.
  • 1947 - Meets Zellig Harris, who introduces Chomsky to the field of theoretical linguistics and convinces him to major in the subject.
  • 1949 - Marries Carol Doris Schatz, a friend since childhood, and was made a Fellow at Harvard.
  • 1955 - Secures a position as assistant professor at MIT, splitting time between teaching linguistics and philosophy and working on a project involving mechanical translation.
  • 1958 - Publishes Syntactic Structures, his first book on linguistics. This is recognized by many as a cornerstone work in developing the scientific study of language.
  • 1967 - Publishes an essay titled "The Responsibility of Intellectuals" criticizing the Vietnam War, of which he was an ardent opponent.
  • 1979 - Collaborated with Edward S. Herman to author Counter-Revolutionary Violence: Bloodbaths in Fact and Propaganda, which was initially refused publication but eventually revised and published. The contents of this work led to his being denounced as an apologist for Pol Pot.
  • 1980 - Chomsky defended the right of Robert Faurisson to advocate Holocaust denial as a part of freedom of speech, and was again condemned as supporting the views of the defendant rather than his rights.

“Citizens of the democratic societies should undertake a course of intellectual self defense to protect themselves from manipulation and control, and to lay the basis for meaningful democracy.”​


-- From Necessary Illusions: Thought Control in Democratic Societies
